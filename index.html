First Post

My best classroom computing experience so far would be working on the final project assignment for Artificial Intelligence last semester. The final project was based on the programming game "Robot Game." We had created a robot for this game early in the semester but it only worked programattically. Now we had to accomplish the same goal by teaching a bot how to play the game through deep learning. The project was very frustrating at first because it seemed like no matter what I tried the robot would not learn anything significant. It took a full two weeks of trying different approaches before my bot had any progress in learning how to play the game. The feeling when I saw the robot play the game correctly and actually kind of well was extremely satisfying since I felt like I had actually learned something.
  Outside of the classroom the first thing that comes to mind is my research into Optical Wireless Communication (OWC) under Dr. Hamza. I have really enjoyed working on this project because it was so challenging. The purpose of this research is to find alternatives to radio frequencies for transmitting information. In this project I'm trying to find a low cost solution to this problem by transmitting a signal through LED's controlled by an arduino and a camera that picks up the transmission. The idea is that the light flickers really fast and the camera should be able to pick up on a message by how the light flashes. In practice this proved to be really difficult since the synchronization between the flicker of the light and the frames per second on the camera had to very precise. A few months ago I finally succeeded with a workable version of the project and although it still has room for improvement I was very proud in getting it to work.

Reflection on Ethcis

My overall impression of the ethics discussion was that it seems that on average we all sort of value the same things. This makes sense since it is nice for a society to have similar values. It makes even more sense when you think about the fact that we all are about the same age had mostly similar upbringings and share a common education in a common area. I'm sure the answers would have had more variety if there was more variety in age and people of different areas.
  I thought it was interesting that a lot of people alluded to the religion they had or were exposed to growing up. It seemed like several people no longer practiced the religion they were exposed to and some outright rejected it now. In my opinion I think you can learn a lot of good things from following a religion but at the same time there are people out there who take advantage of their followers. I think if practiced well even if you don't believe in it you can learn a lot from a religion.

Hive Tracks and Bee Informed Partnership

I think that this discussion ended up in the same area that most tech discussions lead to these days. Since there has been so much advancement and change in technology recently it is hard for legislation and regulation to keep up. I think this has a lot to do with people making legislation not really growing up with these issues and having to learn stuff on the fly. The main issues of how to handle people's information is still a fairly new concept. There is a lot to consider with keeping it safe, keeping it anonymous, and allowing the users to have control over it. There isn't as much concern for an app like Hive Tracks since it is such a niche market but in a more generalized market like Google and Facebook it is a huge issue. These companies have gone from services to advertising companies in the past few years and have essentially turned is into the product. These issues make me kind of worried about what is to come if we don't control what companies can do with our data.

Privacy Policy Reflection

What was interesting to me about the privacy policy I read was that it was pretty easy to understand compared to other legal documents I've looked at. That combined with the fact that Google was pretty upfront about the kind of data they were collecting. There was some language in the policy I read and others we spoke about that does make me uncomfortable. Language that is obviously ambiguous does make you think a little bit more about what these companies are doing with our data. I've never been a huge fan of social media to begin with so I thought I was safe from a lot of data collection. It wasn't until recently that I learned that Google and YouTube, services I use often, were some of the worst offenders for data collection. Because of this I've started using some different browsers and cutting back on YouTube. I really don't like the idea of someone looking over my shoulder all the time so I want to do all I can to ensure my privacy is protected.

Citizen Four Docmuentary

I thought that the Citizen Four documentary was pretty unnerving. I had actually listened to a podcast before that had Edward Snowden on it several years ago where he was talking about the things he exposed in the documentary. Even still it was pretty crazy to me the lengths he went through to not be discovered as the person leaking this information. I think it is a terrible thing to have to live in fear that everything you are doing is being monitored. It does not make me feel safer in the slightest that the NSA has all this power. Maybe it has helped them stop some bad things from happening but this is not for certain and even then it is a very slippery slope. Even though we know now what the NSA is doing and is capable of it doesn't seem like reform to that is coming anytime soon. I like what Edward Snowden did to expose the government but it was all a waste of time if no change comes from it.
